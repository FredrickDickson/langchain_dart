What are the requirements for bail in a murder case under Ghanaian law?

Under the Criminal Procedure Code (Act 30), bail for capital offenses like murder is generally restricted. However, the Supreme Court of Ghana has ruled that bail can be granted in exceptional circumstances based on constitutional rights to personal liberty.

Specifically, in Martin Kpebu v. Attorney General, the court held that the non-bailable nature of certain offenses under Section 96(7) was unconstitutional. Thus, a judge may grant bail if there is no risk of the accused absconding or interfering with investigations.
