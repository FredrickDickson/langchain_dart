Original Query

Is it legal to terminate an employee without notice in Ghana based on recent case law?

check_circle High Confidence (92%)
warning Draft Mode

Executive Summary

Under the Labour Act, 2003 (Act 651), termination without notice is generally prohibited unless specific conditions for summary dismissal are met. Employers are mandated to provide notice or pay in lieu of notice. However, verifiable gross misconduct can justify summary dismissal without notice.

Detailed Analysis

According to Section 15 of the Labour Act, 2003 (Act 651), a contract of employment may be terminated by either party, but reasonable notice must be given [1]. The length of this notice depends on the duration of employment (e.g., monthly contracts require one month's notice).

The only exception permitting termination without notice is "Summary Dismissal" under Section 27. This occurs when the employee has committed a fundamental breach of their duties, such as proven theft, fraud, or habitual absenteeism.

In the case of Nartey-Tokoli & Ors v. V.A.L.C.O, the Supreme Court emphasized that even in summary dismissal, the employer must follow fair procedure and allow the employee a chance to be heard [2]. Failure to do so may result in the termination being deemed "unfair" under Section 63, entitling the employee to compensation.
